Our Solution: School-Based Device Lending + Community Wi-Fi

Two-Part Initiative to Address All Gaps

1. School-Based Device Lending Program

  • Refurbish donated/used laptops/tablets into a lending pool
  • Structured checkout policies with parent/student agreements
  • Device maintenance team (trained TLE teachers + students)
  • Built-in digital literacy training for all borrowers

2. Community Wi-Fi Hotspot Setup

  • Partnership with local government units (LGUs) for free Wi-Fi in high-traffic student areas
  • Coverage optimized for residential zones with high student populations
  • Peak-hour bandwidth management for reliable speeds
  • Guidelines for safe and responsible internet use

Feasibility Check

Yes – the solution aligns with DepEd’s digital learning guidelines, and local officials have committed support for the Wi-Fi initiative. Refurbished devices reduce costs while solving equipment shortages.

Weaknesses & Mitigations

Potential Issues and Action Plans
Weakness Mitigation Strategy
Device damage or loss Require small deposit or parent guarantee; enforce clear usage rules
Slow Wi-Fi during peak hours Partner with ISPs for dedicated bandwidth; schedule usage slots
Lack of digital literacy Integrate training into TLE lessons; create easy-to-follow video tutorials
High maintenance costs Train student "tech aides"; seek corporate sponsorships for repairs
Scheduling conflicts for pickups Offer after-school and weekend pickup windows; set up a simple online booking system
