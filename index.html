The Problem: Digital Inequity in TLE ICT Education

What Is TLE ICT?

Information and Communication Technology (ICT) is a core TLE learning area that equips students with skills for the digital economy—from basic computer operations to coding and digital citizenship. However, critical barriers hold learners back.

Key Challenges

  • Outdated computers and obsolete software
  • Insufficient devices (e.g., 10 working units for 40+ students)
  • Uneven home internet connectivity
  • Lack of device maintenance protocols

Root Cause Analysis (5 Whys Technique)

  1. Why outdated tools? Insufficient budget allocation
  2. Why insufficient budget? ICT is prioritized less than core subjects
  3. Why lower priority? Perception of ICT as a "support subject"
  4. Why this perception? Limited advocacy and impact data
  5. Why limited advocacy? No organized efforts to promote TLE ICT

Impact

Students from underserved schools face skills mismatches, lower employability in tech fields, and reduced motivation. Globally, countries like Germany and South Korea demonstrate that well-equipped TLE programs boost skilled employment rates.
